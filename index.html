When you choose to leave a legacy to IIT Bombay, you are not simply making a gift — you are extending the arc of your own journey. You are investing in minds you may never meet, discoveries yet to be imagined, and a future shaped by courage, curiosity and compassion.
A deeply personal expression

A Living Legacy is a deeply personal expression of gratitude and belief.

Belief in the transformative power of education, research and innovation, expanding opportunity and IIT Bombay's enduring role in shaping thinkers, innovators and nation-builders.

Known globally as bequests or planned giving, the IIT Bombay Living Legacy Programme enables you to designate IIT Bombay and/or the IIT Bombay Heritage Foundation (IITBHF) as a beneficiary in your will, trust, estate plan, and more. It is also designed to maximise the impact of your legacy, often with significant tax advantages.

Your Legacy In Action

IIT Bombay is where India's brightest minds are nurtured, and its most defining ideas take shape. It drives critical research, launches enterprises of global consequence, and advances urgent frontiers like climate science, artificial intelligence, and public health, to name a few. This work is purposeful, pressing, and scaling towards boundless impact. Your legacy ensures the research continues, the questions keep being asked, and the breakthroughs the world needs are never held back.

Sample language

Sample Language for Your Will or Estate Plan

If you choose to include IIT Bombay in your plans, the following sample language can help guide your conversation with your legal advisor.

For US Donors

"I give [an amount equal to X% of the residue of my estate / thereof OR USD ____________ ] to IIT Bombay through IIT Bombay Heritage Foundation, a qualified 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization registered in the State of California, [Tax ID 77-0428724], for its general educational and research purposes."

For India Donors

"I give [an amount equal to X% of the residue of my estate / thereof OR INR ____________ ] to IIT Bombay, for its general educational and research purposes."

The above information is not intended as legal, financial, or tax advice. We encourage you to discuss these options with your attorney.
